Courses At Purdue University

 This is my home page. Still under construction...

Courses:

  1. MA161(cal1): Cal1 is such a breeze. I already knew most of the materials from high school math classes. The only challenge is probally anti-derivative and integrals.
  2. MA162(cal2): Cal2 is pretty hard. Did pretty well at first but not so well on the chapter about sequences. Anyway, I had a really good professor who is very smart and knowledgable.
  3. MA261(cal3): A lot of people say Cal3 is easier than Cal2 but that is not the case to me. Prof. Catlin is a very nice guy and he got his Ph.D from Princeton. However, because of his medical issues, he can't teach well now. I mostly taught myself.
  4. CS180(Java): It is hard for me as someone who has little experience in programming. This is the class that showed me CS is a very hard major.
  5. CS182(Discrete Math): Lots of lots of concepts and logic stuff. I did pretty well the first half, not so well towards the end.
  6. CS240(C,C++): This is the class that makes me to start get interested in Programming. Did so so well all semester except the final. *cry* *cry* Anyway, I still sucks at using gdb to debug.Gustavo is pretty good.
  7. CS250(Computer Architecture): I probably shouldn't choose this class in summer in the first place. So much work with so little help form Prof. Turkstra. I have so many problems with him not giving enough information about course material in lectures.
  8. CS251(Data Structures and Algorithm): Gustavo is awesome! Did learn a lot from this class!
  9. PHYS172(Mordern Mechanics): Okay. College Physics is hard... but kinda fun
  10. ECON252(Macroeconomics): Such an easy class. Management and Econ stuff are typically pretty easy, I guess.
  11. ENGL106(First Year Composition): Glad I took it with PPI first year.
  12. SPAN101(Elementry Spanish): Something that I always want to try. Me gusta Espanol.
  13. CS471(Introduction to Artificial Intelligence): in progress...
