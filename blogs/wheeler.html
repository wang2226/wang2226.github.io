Summit Wheeler Peak in Nevada

Wheeler Peak came onto my radar last summer when I traveled U.S. 50, a.k.a. the Loniest Road in America. I did not summit last year since I got there really late and only hiked to Stella Lake. This year, I came back to finish my summit dream. This is probably the most accessible 13ers, the trailhead starts at around 10,000 ft and almost reach the tree line. I camped in the back of my car at the trailhead. This is my first time sleeping at high elevation. I had to crack the front windows for more ventilation because I was having some hard time breathing in my car.

The sun woke me up around 6:30, I had some breakfast, packed all my gear and headed out for the summit. The first portion of the trail is to clear the treeline, the trail is nice and gradual. After about 2.1 miles, I finally reached the treeline. However, at this point, I have only gained about 1,000 vert, which means the rest of the trail is going to be really steep.

After the treeline, the rest of the trail is on an endless scree field. To reach the summit, I had to traverse a ridgeline, and then scramble all the way up to the summit. The scree field is pretty technical, the footing is very important as nothing is stable under my feet. Also, as the elevation started to get above 11,000 ft, I could feel it started to have a toll on my body. I started to go a lot slower and have difficulty of breathing smoothly. The trail started to get steeper and steeper, and the summit seemed impossible to reach.

Finally, after I made it to the end of the ridge, I can see the summit is within reach. However, at this point, I still have 1,000 vert to go and it is really steep and technical. I took a longer break, and had a fruit bar and energy gel to prepare for the final push to the summit. The final push to the summit required steep scrambling. Also, when I was about 100 ft below the summit, I lost the trail. I had to do some really mad scrambling on loose boulders, it was quite nerve wracking. Once I reached the summit, I found a mailbox that contains a log book and a sign. Overall it was quite an accomplishment being able to summit a 13er.
