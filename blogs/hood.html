Hike and Ski at Mt Hood

Summer skiing on Mt Hood had been on my bucket list for a while ever since I knew that it offers year-round skiing. Since I have to get up really early to get the best snow condition, I decide to drive up to Hood, hike and camp for the night. For the hike, I decide to hike up to Illumination rock, which is a saddle sitting below the summit. Even though it is late June, the moutain is still covered in deep snow and ice, hiking up anywhere pass the lodge requires crampons. I started my hike from the lodge, following the climber's trail. The hike is pretty straight forward, just keep going up along the ski lifts right out of the ski area boundary. I started out around 6,000 ft, it was pretty warm, so I have shorts on and traverse the snowfield.

After gained about 1,500 ft elevation, the temperature started to drop and the slope got steeper. I put on pants and crampons. The view to Mt Jefferson was amazing. I love these snow capped volcanoes, you can see miles from them. Along the trail, I found a container full of salt. It turns out those are rock salt, the salt works by essentially melting the snow crystals at the very top of the ski surface, creating a slurry that quickly refreezes as a smoother, faster skiing surface. The snow almost instantly becomes less “grabby.” The rock salt is a mineral which exists naturally and does not affect the environment. That's the secret of year-round skiing at Hood.

After a long and streneous climb, I passed the last ski lift of Palmer and reached the saddle of Illumination Rock. I am really glad I have my mountaineering and crampons, they definitely made the steep section a lot easier. From there, I started descending and got back to my car around 6:30. I camped at Still Creek for the night.

I woke up around 4:50 the next morning and drove to the parking lot of Timberline Lodge. Had breakfast and a cup of coffee while watching the sunrise at Hood. The lift ticket office opened at 6:30, and the lift opened at 7. To my surprise, it was the longest lift line I have ever seen. There are many camps from all over the country. I waited for about 40 minutes to get on my first chair.

For summer skiing, only two runs are open, magic mile, which is a blue run, the other one is Palmer, which is a black run. I don't know if it is because of summer skiing or something else, the blue run feels like a green, and the black run feels like an easy blue. The snow was pretty good up top, but it got slushy towards the bottom. I skied 6 runs, mainly because the lift line was really long. Overall, it was quite a unique experience. Hood never disappoints me.
