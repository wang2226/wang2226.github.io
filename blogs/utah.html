Ski Utah

Skied Snowbird, Alta, Deer Valley, Park City and Powder Mountain in Utah. Checked them off my list from the book "100 Slopes of a Lifetime" by National Geography. Experienced my very first snowcat-skiing off lightening ridge at powder mountain.

Snowbird is perhaps my favorite out of the 5 resorts that I visited in Utah. It has beatiful views with amazing facilities. The snow is incredibly good and the crowd is managable considering its size.

Alta is one of the oldest ski resort in the country. It is truely special to experince the vintage double chair and the great terrain it has to offer. I was lucky that the day ended being an awesome powder dump towards the end.

Deer Vallye is a fancy resort with great services. The grooming is top notch, but it is overly crowded.

Park City is massive. It takes at least two or three days to explore the entire resort.

Powder Mountain is very underrated. This place has great snow, variety of terrain and virtually no crowd.
